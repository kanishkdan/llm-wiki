# Belief System

Core principles and mental models that define Kanishk's life outlook. Originally written around August 2023.

## On Online Presence
- **Game to be played:** Followers drive attention to products and revenue. Treat gaining followers as a game you can learn.
- **Discovery focus:** Writing should ideally be about discovery and original thought.

## Work and Execution
- **Fight the hard battles:** Take on insurmountable problems with disproportionate rewards. Surprise yourself.
- **Don't assume context:** Be as descriptive and contextual about your thoughts as possible.
- **Ruthless feedback:** If you don't like something, share it and get it fixed. Solving it yourself is even better.
- **Avoid lazy work:** Superficial work is easy to get away with but non-lazy work wins in the long run.
- **Avoid FU responses:** Don't use lazy "not in line with strategy" dismissals. Explain the "why."
- **Don't get followed up with:** Aim to be someone who never needs a reminder. It's a testament to planning.
- **Flow state:** Repeatedly doing something until you unlock "auto-pilot" mode (e.g., gym, coding).

## Communication and Relationships
- **Cut the small talk:** Focus on deep talk. Favorite question: "If money wasn't a problem, what would you spend the rest of your life doing?"
- **Disagreements:** Ask if a disagreement is worth halting progress. Voice it, but keep things moving unless it's critical.
- **Getting things done through others:** Make it easier for others to help you. Show your investment and provide full context (e.g., provide specific data tables to developers).
- **Don't debate with family:** Nobody wins a family debate; it's like Monopoly.
- **Oration vs Substance:** Oration is overrated; substance is underrated.

## Life Choices
- **Try things young:** Quickly check off things you don't want to do for the rest of your life.
- **Alcohol:** Peer pressure is best handled by simply saying you've stopped for a long time. Fun comes from the company, not the drink.
- **Walk away from drama:** Cutting out drama (work, friends) leaves time for the mind to wander.
